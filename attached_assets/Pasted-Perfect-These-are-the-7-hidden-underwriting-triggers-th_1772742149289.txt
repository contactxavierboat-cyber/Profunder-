1. Inquiry Velocity (Credit Seeking Behavior)

Too many recent inquiries signals financial stress or credit stacking.

Typical lender tolerance:

• 0–2 inquiries / 90 days → safe
• 3–5 inquiries / 90 days → caution
• 6+ inquiries / 90 days → high denial probability

Example your engine could show:

Velocity Risk: High
7 inquiries in last 60 days

⸻

2. Revolver Utilization Spikes

Even one card near max can trigger denial.

Example:

Card 1: $10k limit
Balance: $8,700

Utilization = 87%

Even if overall utilization looks fine, banks see card-level risk.

Your engine should flag:

Revolver Concentration Risk

⸻

3. Thin Primary File

Authorized user accounts can inflate score but not trust.

Banks prefer:

• 5–8 primary accounts

Risk trigger:

• 3 or fewer primary tradelines

Your UI already references this — which is good.

⸻

4. Low Limit Profile

Banks look at existing lender confidence.

Example:

Credit score: 740
Cards:

• $500
• $800
• $1200

Even with good score, lenders think:

Other banks don’t trust this borrower with real limits.

Approval likelihood drops.

⸻

5. New Account Clustering

Too many new accounts recently signals risk.

Typical lender tolerance:

• 0–1 new accounts in 6 months

Risk trigger:

• 3+ new accounts in 6 months

Your engine could show:

Account Age Stability: Weak

⸻

6. Profile Asymmetry

Banks like balanced credit structures.

Ideal structure:

• Revolvers (credit cards)
• Installment (loan)

If someone only has credit cards:

Underwriting models reduce approval probability.

Your UI already mentions:

1 primary installment account

Which is correct.

⸻

7. Synthetic Profile Signals

Rare but serious.

Example triggers:

• Many authorized user accounts
• Rapid tradeline additions
• Large limits suddenly appearing

This can trigger fraud or synthetic identity checks.

Your engine could flag:

Profile Integrity Risk
