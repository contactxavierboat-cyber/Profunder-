For your Capital Readiness / Funding Strategy engine, the “perfect” profile for first-round funding is not the same as a perfect credit score. Lenders care about structure, stability, and capacity. A profile can be 760+ but still be unfundable if the structure is weak.

Below is what most lenders consider “fundable” for a first approval stack.”

⸻

Ideal First-Round Fundable Profile

1. Revolving Credit Structure
	•	3–5 primary credit cards
	•	Each card $5,000 – $15,000 limit
	•	Highest limit $10k+

Why:
Banks use limit matching. If your highest card is $10k, new approvals often fall between $6k–$12k.

⸻

2. Revolver Utilization
	•	1–9% utilization ideal
	•	Under 30% acceptable
	•	No individual card above 30%

Example ideal:

Card	Limit	Balance
Amex	$12k	$200
Chase	$9k	$150
Citi	$7k	$100

Total utilization ≈ 2–3%

This signals low risk and financial control.

⸻

3. Account Age

Average age of accounts:
	•	3+ years ideal
	•	Oldest account 5+ years

New profiles trigger risk algorithms.

⸻

4. Tradeline Depth

Ideal structure:
	•	3–5 revolving accounts
	•	1–2 installment loans

Installment examples:
	•	auto loan
	•	student loan
	•	personal loan

Why: lenders like profile balance.

⸻

5. Inquiry Velocity

This is one of the most important funding metrics.

Ideal:
	•	0–2 inquiries in last 6 months
	•	0–3 inquiries in 12 months

Too many inquiries = credit seeking behavior.

⸻

6. Payment History

Must be:
	•	100% on-time
	•	No collections
	•	No charge-offs
	•	No recent late payments

Even one recent 30-day late can destroy approvals.

⸻

7. Credit Limit Strength

Total revolving limits ideally:

$25k – $60k+

Example ideal first-round profile:

Card	Limit
Amex	$12k
Chase	$10k
Citi	$8k
Discover	$6k

Total = $36k limits

This tells lenders:

Other banks trust this borrower.

⸻

8. No Red Flags

Avoid:
	•	heavy authorized user reliance
	•	maxed out cards
	•	recent account openings
	•	excessive inquiries
	•	thin files

⸻
