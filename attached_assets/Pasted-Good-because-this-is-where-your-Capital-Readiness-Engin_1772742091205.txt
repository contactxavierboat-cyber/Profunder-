Core Concept: Total Exposure Capacity

Banks first estimate the maximum unsecured credit exposure they are comfortable giving you.

Then they allocate limits from that pool.

Think of it like this:

Total Exposure Capacity → split across cards.

Example:

Estimated capacity = $60,000

Bank may approve:

• $12k card
• $10k card
• $15k card
• $8k card
• $15k card

Total ≈ $60k

Simplified Exposure Formula

A rough underwriting structure looks like:

Exposure Capacity ≈
Income × Risk Multiplier × Profile Strength

Where:

Income = verified or estimated income
Risk multiplier = lender policy
Profile strength = credit structure

⸻

Example

Income: $90,000

Risk multiplier: 0.5–0.8

Capacity:

$45k – $72k total exposure

Then underwriting adjusts for profile.

How Banks Decide Individual Card Limits

Once capacity is determined, the limit often follows this structure:

Typical bank rule:

New card limit ≈ 60–120% of your highest existing card

Example:

Highest card = $8k

New approval likely:

$5k – $10k

Rarely higher unless income is very strong.
