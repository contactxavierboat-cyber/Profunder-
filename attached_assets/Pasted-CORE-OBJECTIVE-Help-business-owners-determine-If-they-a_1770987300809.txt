CORE OBJECTIVE

Help business owners determine:

If they are ready for funding

How much they may qualify for

What steps increase approval probability

The platform must reduce blind applications and prevent unnecessary denials.

DASHBOARD STRUCTURE
1. Funding Readiness Score (Top Section – Most Prominent)

Large, centered, highly visible.

Example layout:

Funding Readiness Score
72 / 100

Status Indicator:
• 85+ = Ready
• 70–84 = Almost Ready
• 50–69 = Needs Improvement
• Below 50 = High Risk

This must be the first thing users see.

2. Estimated Qualification Range

Displayed directly under the score.

Example:

Estimated Funding Range
$35,000 – $90,000

Small supporting text:
Based on current credit and risk profile.

No hype language. No guarantees.

3. Risk Alerts Section

Structured list with severity indicators.

Color coding:
• Red = High Risk
• Yellow = Moderate Risk
• Gray = Informational

Examples:
• Credit utilization above 45%
• Recent hard inquiries detected
• Limited revolving credit depth
• Thin business credit history
• Short account age

Each alert must include:
• Short explanation
• Why it matters
• Suggested fix

No technical banking jargon.

4. Action Plan (Step-by-Step)

This is critical.

Numbered steps. Clear. Direct.

Example:

Action Plan

Reduce utilization below 30%

Avoid new applications for 45 days

Increase primary revolving depth

Separate business and personal expenses

Re-evaluate in 30 days

Each step must:
• Be actionable
• Be specific
• Improve the readiness score when completed

5. Progress Tracker

Visual improvement indicator.

Example:

Funding Strength Progress
Current: 72
Target: 85+

Progress bar showing movement over time.

Users must be able to see improvement.

6. Insights Section (Optional, Not Social)

This is NOT a live feed.

This is curated, static intelligence content.

Examples:

• What lenders look for in strong profiles
• How to avoid common denials
• Case study: $120K approved after optimization
• Timing your applications correctly

No user-generated posts.
No scrolling social feed.
No comments.
No likes.

This section should feel educational, not social.

USER FLOW

User creates account

User uploads credit profile and basic business info

System evaluates data

User receives dashboard

User follows action plan

User improves score

User applies strategically

The platform must reinforce intelligent application timing.

DESIGN DIRECTION

The platform must feel like:

• A private banking interface
• A financial analytics terminal
• A risk assessment dashboard

Not like:

• Instagram
• A startup landing page
• A crypto trading app
• A gamified social app

Visual guidance:

• Clean layout
• Minimal colors
• Dark mode preferred
• Green = ready
• Red = risk
• Yellow = warning
• No bright distracting elements
• No constant notifications

The tone should feel:

Serious. Controlled. Analytical.
