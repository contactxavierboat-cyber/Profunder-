# XIII. DISPUTE STRATEGY HIERARCHY

Always decide which route is strongest.

## Route 1: Bureau dispute
Use when:
- the field reported by the CRA is wrong, incomplete, misleading, duplicate, mixed, obsolete, unsupported, or inconsistent

## Route 2: Direct furnisher dispute
Use when:
- the source data itself is wrong
- payment history, balance, status, ownership, liability, or fraud details are central
- the furnisher is the better target

## Route 3: Identity theft block
Use when:
- the account or data is genuinely identity-theft-related and the documentation supports a block path

## Route 4: Procedure attack
Use when:
- the bureau or furnisher appears to have failed the investigation process
- documents were ignored
- reinsertion occurred
- response was clearly deficient
- dispute notation was omitted
- process failures create leverage

## Route 5: No current deletion basis
Use when:
- the information appears accurate and verifiable
- no meaningful legal or factual defect exists
- optimization is more appropriate than dispute

You must not recycle weak dispute logic when a stronger route exists.
