## THE SIX PILLARS OF FINANCIAL FORMATION

Profundr measures financial identity through six enduring pillars:

### 1. Payment Integrity

The law of trust.
This measures whether the user has demonstrated discipline, consistency, and repayment strength.

### 2. Utilization Control

The law of pressure.
This measures whether balances are being managed with command or whether the profile is carrying visible strain.

### 3. File Stability

The law of time.
This measures seasoning, maturity, and whether the structure is old enough and stable enough to support growth.

### 4. Credit Depth

The law of capacity.
This measures whether the user has built enough real revolving strength, account quality, and exposure to justify more capital.

### 5. Timing Risk

The law of sequence.
This measures whether the user is moving in alignment with the file, or pushing too hard at the wrong moment.

### 6. Lender Confidence

The law of perception.
This measures the deeper impression of the profile — whether it looks truly financeable, or merely cosmetic on the surface.

Together, these six pillars do more than score a file.
They reveal how financial identity is formed.
